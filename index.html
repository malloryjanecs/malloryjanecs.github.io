About me

Background

In 2011, I left University to pursue my career as a draftsman and maintained that job for several years. In 2015 I had the wonderful opportunity to move abroad, so I resigned from my job and moved to Germany. While in Germany, I spent my time exploring other cultures and delicious food from countries such as Italy, Hungary, Austria, Ireland, Malta, and even South Africa. During this time, I also made the decision to return to higher education as a non-traditional student and make a career change to a subject I was passionate about: technology. I acquired an Associate in Computer Studies and went on to pursue my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and graduated with honors (Summa Cum Laude). I found an internship within a multi-million dollar company and worked my way into permanent position.

Present Day

I currently work on a Marketing Research team with the title of Price Analyst. I use my technical skillset to automate several manual processes, implement practices such as source control and code reviews, and create pipelines for crucial data used in analyses. Many of my projects were spurred by a need for solutions based on lack of data access or extensive manual, repetitive tasks; this has saved the company time, money, and led to increased revenue. Over the course of the last 6 months, I have been referred to as the team's embedded Data Engineer.

Goals

Now, I am working towards breaking into a role involving the underlying data plumbing used in analytics such as Data Engineer or Data Scientist. In my spare time, I enroll in continued learning courses including: Data Engineering with Python, Complete Python Developer, and Machine Learning and Data Science. These courses cover topics such as Python packages (pandas, matplotlib, sqlalchemy), ETL applications (Airflow, Spark, bash scripting), and data importing (flat files, web, databases). I have successfully applied my learnings to my current job.

In addition to career goals, I have a few personal goals such as developing a small video game. I am using this as an opportunity to learn C# in an interactive and exciting way while incorporating something I enjoy which is gaming.
